I would never write a paper again using Word - except I would be forced, too, what will happen, I think. But similarly, I don’t want to write papers using Latex - too many curly braces.

Best of both worlds: Markdown. Comes with “builtin” R.

Here’s an example how to do scholarly citation in markdown.

In-Text citation

Use this notation, to cite some book or paper or whatever:

The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly face similarly difficulties [@thedude2012].

This sentence will be rendered to something like:

The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly face similarly difficulties (Dude, 2012).

The format will depend on your chosen formation style, see below.
